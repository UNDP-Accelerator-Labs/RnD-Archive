# Systems leadership

```
## Overview
Systems leadership is a skill that enables individuals to bring together diverse stakeholders and facilitate collective action towards a shared goal.

## Purpose
The purpose of systems leadership is to convene network leaders, work towards a common intention, and enable self-organized or distributed actions.

## How it works
This skill involves creating an environment where multiple actors can collaborate and take ownership of their roles in achieving a desired outcome. It requires the ability to navigate complex relationships, build trust, and facilitate collective decision-making.

## Requirements
Effective systems leadership demands strong interpersonal skills, emotional intelligence, and the capacity to adapt to changing circumstances.

## Reflections
Systems leaders must be willing to let go of control and empower others to take ownership of their actions. This requires a high degree of self-awareness, humility, and trust in the collective ability to achieve a shared goal.

## Related methods (if any)
Co-creation, Collaborative governance

## License
No specific license is required for systems leadership, as it is a skill that can be developed through practice and experience.

## Examples
Examples of successful systems leadership include initiatives such as the 100 Resilient Cities program or the Rockefeller Foundation's efforts to build resilience in cities worldwide.
```
